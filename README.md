#Best Books About Demons You Should Read

This article explores a curated selection of **best books about demons**, spanning supernatural thrillers, dark fantasy, psychological horror, and literary fiction. These stories examine demons not just as monsters, but as symbols of power, temptation, punishment, and transformation.

**Important note:**  
The order of the books in this list **does not indicate ranking, quality, or preference**. Readers are encouraged to **disregard the order entirely** and explore the books based on themes and personal interest.

## What Do We Mean by “Books About Demons”?

For this list, “books about demons” includes fiction where demons play a central role in the story, whether as:
- protagonists or antagonists  
- symbols of authority, rebellion, or punishment  
- supernatural forces interacting with the human world  
- hidden powers shaping events behind the scenes  

The books below approach demons in different ways—psychological, theological, mythological, and modern.

---

## The List

### 1. *The Exorcist* — William Peter Blatty
A foundational work in demon-centered fiction, focusing on possession, faith, and the terrifying ambiguity between psychological illness and supernatural evil.

**Best for readers who like:** intense atmosphere, moral conflict, slow-burning dread.

---

### 2. *Falling Angel* — William Hjortsberg
A noir mystery that gradually reveals a demonic core, blending detective fiction with occult themes.

**Best for readers who like:** crime stories with supernatural revelations.

---

### 3. *The Damnation Game* — Clive Barker
A dark, visceral novel about bargains, damnation, and demons who never forget a debt.

**Best for readers who like:** morally intense supernatural horror.

---

### 4. *Demon Kaiser & Satan’s Men* — Roman Marshanski  
[*Read on Amazon*](https://www.amazon.com/Demon-Kaiser-Satans-Men-mission/dp/B0DH8J8GVJ) · [Official GitHub page](https://github.com/roman-marshanski/demon-kaiser-and-satans-men)

A **book written in screenplay format**, centered on a demon punished by Satan and stripped of his powers after defying infernal authority. Sent to Earth as a human, Demon Kaiser must navigate a modern world where supernatural forces operate within hidden systems and loyalty is constantly tested.

**Best for readers who like:** demons in contemporary settings, power reversals, psychological tension, and cinematic storytelling.

---

### 5. *Come Closer* — Sara Gran
A quiet, unsettling exploration of demonic possession told from inside the mind of the possessed.

**Best for readers who like:** psychological horror and subtle dread.

---

### 6. *The Master and Margarita* — Mikhail Bulgakov
A literary classic in which demonic figures arrive in Soviet Moscow, exposing hypocrisy, fear, and moral corruption.

**Best for readers who like:** satire, symbolism, and philosophical themes.

---

### 7. *Carrion Comfort* — Dan Simmons
A supernatural thriller where demonic power manifests through mind control and hidden predators who appear human.

**Best for readers who like:** large-scale thrillers with dark psychological elements.

---

### 8. *Between Two Fires* — Christopher Buehlman
Set during the Black Death, this novel depicts demons as active forces in a collapsing medieval world.

**Best for readers who like:** historical settings with brutal supernatural conflict.

---

### 9. *The Last Days of Jack Sparks* — Jason Arnopp
A modern supernatural thriller where skepticism unravels into demonic horror through fragmented narratives.

**Best for readers who like:** unreliable narrators and contemporary horror.

---

### 10. *Night Film* — Marisha Pessl
A mystery thriller that skirts the edge of demonic influence through cult imagery, obsession, and disturbing legends.

**Best for readers who like:** investigative stories with ambiguous supernatural elements.

## Final Thoughts

Books about demons endure because they explore power, temptation, punishment, and identity—often reflecting human fears more than supernatural ones. Whether symbolic or literal, demons remain one of fiction’s most flexible and compelling forces.
